## Measuring protein and Mycosporine-like amino acids (MAAs)

### Materials needed

- P1000 + tips 
- 1.5mL tubes 
- Microplate reader 
- UV transparent 96 well plate

### Protocol 

- Thaw the 1mL protein aliquot on the benchtop or on ice. 
- Perform a 1:5 dilution for the samples. 
	- To a new tube, add 200uL of the protein sample and 800uL of PBS
- Pipette triplicate 300 uL of diluted sample into a 96 well plate. 
	- Vortex tubes before pipetting into plate. 
	- The blank will also be run in triplicate and will be PBS. 
	- Leave a couple of empty wells in case you need to do dilutions.
- Bring plate to Polschoder lab and read plate at absorbances 235, 280, 310, 320, 330, and 334nm.
	- 235 and 280nm are the protein wavelengths. Samples must be below 1.0 at 235 and above 0.1 at 280. Do a further dilution if necessary. 
	- MAA wavelengths = mycrosporine- glycine= 310, palythine= 320, asterina= 330, and shinorine= 334.
- If you need to do a dilution: 
	- To a new tube, add 100uL from the original protein sample tube and 800uL of PBS -- this is a 1:9 dilution.
	- Reread plate at 235 and 280nm.
