## Guava for symbiont cell counting

These protocols were developed by members of the Barott lab (Teegan Innis, Luella Allen-Waller, Ben Glass, Kristin Brown, Marcelina Martynek). This post will contain details on using the Guava for cell counting for the KBay samples for the NSF CAREER project. 

### Materials needed 

- Guava + computer (upstairs on Barott lab bench)
- Guava Instrument Cleaning Fluid (ICF)
- Bleach
- DI water 
- 96 well plate 
- p200 + tips 
- Guava tubes 
- Syringe 

### Guava overview 

The Guava flow cytometer detects and counts particles passing through a microcapillary tube on the basis of their fluorescence and light scattering. 

### Protocol 

This protocol will use samples generated during airbrushing (see airbrushing post [here](https://github.com/JillAshey/Ashey_Barott_Lab_Notebook/blob/main/posts/2025-11-11-KBay-Airbrushing.md)). 

- Before starting, refill the ICF container on the front right of the Guava (has a blue showerhead on the container). Empty the Guava waste (has a red crossbones on the container). 
- Turn on the Guava (switch on the back right) and the associated laptop. 
- Once both the Guava and laptop are on, open the GuavaSoft software 
- Click Cleaning
- Click Start Clean -- this will open the Guava door for you to load the tubes for cleaning. 
- Using the Guava tubes (in the second drawer to the left of the computer chair), arrange the tubes as such: 

![](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JillAshey/Ashey_Barott_Lab_Notebook/refs/heads/main/images/Guava/cleaning_tube_layout.png)

- This tray message will be on the screen - it is essentially telling you how to arrange the tubes and asking for a capillary "park" position. Click on the circle next to 9 for the park position and then click OK. 

![](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JillAshey/Ashey_Barott_Lab_Notebook/refs/heads/main/images/Guava/guava_clean.JPG)

Cleaning will take ~15 mins. 

- After cleaning is done, click main menu in the top right hand corner. 
- Click InCyte to begin sample processing. The screen will look like this:

![](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JillAshey/Ashey_Barott_Lab_Notebook/refs/heads/main/images/Guava/guava_run.JPG)

- To start, click "Edit Worklist" -- the scroll with the pencil at the top left hand corner of the screen.
- This will open the worklist editor. Highlight the wells that you have samples in for your plate and click "Acquire this sample" under sample specific. Do not make any edits to the settings.

![](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JillAshey/Ashey_Barott_Lab_Notebook/refs/heads/main/images/Guava/worklist_editor.JPG)

- Click "Run Worklist". This will open a window for you to set the settings for your samples of interest. 
	- Under Dataset: save a fcs file with the date to your folder on the computer. 
	- Under Analysis Method: use the `Training_Method.gsy` document. As of 6/11/26, it lives at `C:/Users/User/Box Sync (gbe@upenn.edu)/Barott Lab Guava/Barott Lab 2020/Training_Method.gsy`. 
	- Under Settings: use the `SYM count settings.gst` document. As of 6/11/26, it lives at `C:/Users/User/Box Sync (gbe@upenn.edu)/Barott Lab Guava/Barott Lab 2020/SYM count settings.gst`.
	- Don't do anything for the Compensation. 
- After the settings and analysis method are selected, click "Acquire".  
- The door will open at some point. Put your 96-well plate on the designated platform and arrange the Guava tubes for the runs as such:

![](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JillAshey/Ashey_Barott_Lab_Notebook/refs/heads/main/images/Guava/samples_tube_layout.png)

Hooray samples should be running now! For each sample, the Guava is set to acquire at least 5000 "events" (ie cells) or to run for 3mins and 30 secs, whichever comes first. Here is what the screen should look like if the Guava is running well: 

![](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JillAshey/Ashey_Barott_Lab_Notebook/refs/heads/main/images/Guava/guava_actual_run.JPG)

I like to sit on my computer with the Guava while it runs in case it clogs (see below).

- Once the run is complete, save the data as a CSV by clicking "Show Group Stats" on the left hand side bar. You can then export the data as a CSV. 
- Close the program, which will return you to the main menu. To get the samples out, click Cleaning and then Eject Tray. 
- Remove and throw away the plate and the tubes. Click load tray to close the door. 
- Close the Guava software and turn off the Guava machine. 

#### What to do if the Guava clogs

The Guava flow cell can become clogged if something gets caught in it or the cells are clumpy. If this happens, you will notice it because the number of "events" starts to slow way down so its only capturing one or two events. If this happens, do the following: 

- Pause the worklist using the Pause button at the top left of the screen. Note what sample/well the Guava clogged so you can remove it from downstream analysis. 
- Remove the silver square on the top of the Guava. This will give you access to the flow cell. 
- Carefully unscrew the flow cell from the Guava (the screw on the right). Once its unscrewed, remove the flow cell by carefully pulling it straight up. 
- Set the flow cell to the side on a flat surface on a kim wipe. Make sure the capillary is not touching anything. 
- Unscrew the cap to the DI water squeeze bottle and pour some DI water into the cap. 
- Suck up ~1mL of DI water into the small syringe. 
- Screw the head of the small syringe onto the flow cell. 
- Standing over a sink or trash can, depress the syringe until you can see a small stream of water coming out of the flow cell. Hooray no more clog! 
- Put the flow cell back in the Guava and screw it back into the side. 

Be super careful when handling the flow cell. It's very expensive and fragile so we want to keep it in good condition.
