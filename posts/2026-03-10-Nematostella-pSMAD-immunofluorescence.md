## Immunofluorescence of pSMAD in Nematostella larvae

This post contains the protocol for immunostaining in Nematostella embryos and larvae. We will be using pSMAD as our protein of interest for immunofluorescence. We will be using a modified protocol from [Knabl et al. 2024](https://elifesciences.org/articles/80803#s4): 

""Immunostainings in Nematostella were performed according to Leclère and Rentzsch, 2014 with minor changes. Embryos were fixed for 2 min in ice-cold 0.25% glutaraldehyde/3.7% formaldehyde/PTX (1× PBS, 0.3% Triton-X), then transferred to ice-cold 3.7% FA/PTX and fixed at 4°C for 1 hr. The samples were washed 5× with PTX, incubated in ice-cold methanol for 8 min, and washed 3× with PTX. After 2 hr in the blocking solution containing 5% heat-inactivated sheep serum/95% PTX-BSA (PTX-BSA = 1% BSA/PTX), the samples were incubated overnight with the primary antibody (rabbit anti-pSMAD1/5/9 [Cell Signaling, #13820; 1:200] or rat αNvHoxE antibody [1:500]) in blocking solution. Embryos were then washed 5× with PTX, blocked for 2 hr in blocking solution, and incubated overnight with secondary antibody (goat α-rabbit IgG-Alexa568, goat α-rabbit IgG-Alexa633 or goat α-rat IgG-DyLight488) and DAPI (1:1000) in blocking solution. After the secondary antibody staining, the samples were washed 5× with PTX, mounted in Vectashield (VectorLabs), and imaged under Leica SP5X or Leica SP8 confocal microscopes."

This is Kelsey's protocol for staining Aiptasia larvae: "In the fume hood, 500 μLs of larvae in 4% formaldehyde were transferred to four wells of a 12-well plate, one well for each treatment group.  1 mL of PBSTx was added to each well, and the plate was placed on a shaker set to low for five minutes.  After five minutes, the larvae were allowed to settle, and one mL of wash was removed from each well.  This was repeated with two more washes of PBSTx.  After the final wash, larvae were allowed to settle, and as much liquid as possible, without disturbing the larvae, was removed from each well with a glass pasteur pipette.  Without allowing the larvae to dry out, 1.5 mLs of blocking buffer was added to each well.  Larvae were set to incubate overnight in blocking buffer with gentle shaking at 4 degrees Celsius.  The next day, as much blocking buffer as possible was removed from each well.  700 μLs fo 1:100 or 1:250 concentration of primary rabbit-anti-coral sAC antibody were added to two designated wells, and 700 μLs of blocking buffer were added to no primary control wells.  Larvae were incubated overnight at 4 degrees Celsius with gentle shaking.  The next day, as much primary antibody/blocking buffer treatment as possible was removed by glass pasteur pipette, and each well of larvae was washed with 1 mL of PBSTx four times for 10 minutes each with gentle shaking at room temperature.  The final wash of PBSTx was removed, and 700 μLs of 1:1000 goat-anti-rabbit-AlexaFluor555 (diluted in blocking buffer) were added to designated wells.  No-secondary controls received 700 μLs of blocking buffer.  From this point, larvae were kept in the dark as much as possible to prevent bleaching of the flurophore.  Larvae were incubated in secondary treatment for two hours at room temperature with gentle shaking.  After incubation, secondary antibody/blocking buffer was removed, and wells containing larvae were once again washed with 1 mL of PBSTx four times for 10 minutes each with gentle shaking at room temperature.  After the fourth wash, a final wash of 10 minutes with 1 mL of PBS was performed at room temperature with gentle shaking.  500 μLs of PBS were removed from each well.  Larvae from each well were removed by a p200 with a cut-off tip and placed on designated slides.  Excess liquid was removed from slides by glass pasteur pipette without disturbing larvae.  One drop of mounting media (ProLongTM Glass Antifade Mountant with NucBlue TM Stain adjusted to room temperature) was placed directly onto each puddle of larvae.  Slides were sett to cure overnight in the dark at room temperature before placing coverslips.  The next day, a drop of immersion oil was applied onto the cured mounting media, then coverslips were placed and sealed with nailpolish."

### Materials

* Transfer pipettes, pipettes, pipette tips 
* 1.5mL, 15mL, 50mL tubes
* 6 or 24 well plates 
* Shaker 
* 4C cold room -- where is this?
* 4C fridge 
* Foil
* Mounting media (Prolong Gold antifade with DAPI)
* Glass pipettes + bulbs
* Microscope slides + coverslips 
* Confocal 
* Immersion oil 
* Solutions / reagents 
	* 4% PFA
	* Filtered seawater 
	* 1x PBS 
	* Triton-X 100 
	* BSA (solid)
	* KHL (solid)
	* Normal goat serum 
	* Rabbit anti-pSMAD1/5/9 [Cell Signaling, #13820; 1:200] for primary antibody
	* Alexa666 for secondary antibody 

## Protocol 

**NOTE**: When moving fixed larvae, use a P200 with tip cut off to avoid shearing them. 

### Making reagents and solutions 

- PTX (wash buffer) - 1x PBS and Triton-X 100 
	- Triton-X is super viscous and difficult to pipette. Make a 10% Triton-X 100 stock solution. To do this, add 5mL of Triton and 45mL of 1x PBS to a 50mL falcon tube. Place tube on a rocker at low speed for ~2hrs or until solution is clear. 
	- To make 100mL of 0.3% PTX wash buffer, add 97mL of 1x PBS and 3mL of 10% Triton stock. To make 50mL, add 48.5mL of 1x PBS and 1.5 mL of 10% Triton stock.
	- This can be stored at room temperature.  
- KHL (Keyhole Limpet Hemocyanin) stock solution -- Kelsey added this in the blocking buffer for the Aiptasia larvae. 
	- Add 1mL of 1x PBS and 0.01g of solid KHL to a 1.5mL tube. 
	- Flick to mix and put in fridge overnight to hydrate.
	- In the morning, centrifuge tube at 10000g for 10 mins at 4°C. Move the supernatent to new tube and discard pellet. 
	- Store at -20°C. This is sensitive to freeze-thaw cycles, so make aliquots if needed. 
- Blocking buffer 
	- Add 18mL of 1x PBS and 600uL of 10% Triton stock to 50mL tube.
	- Add 0.2g of solid BSA and invert until dissolved (do not vortext). 
	- Add 1mL of normal goat serum (NGS) and 400uL of KHL solution.
	- This should be made the same day as use. If it cannot be made the same day, add the NGS and KHL right before adding the larvae.  
- Primary antibody (1:200)
	- Add 597uL of blocking buffer and 3uL of primary antibody. Thaw antibodu on ice and spin down before using. 
	- Make the same day as use. 
- Secondary antibody (1:1000)
	- Add 999uL of blocking buffer and 1uL of secondary antibody (Alexa666). 
	- Make same day as use. 
	- The secondary antibody is light sensitive! When working with it, turn lights off. 

### Fixation 

- Fix larvae in 1mL of 4% PFA for 1hr at room temperature. See fixation protocol [here](https://github.com/JillAshey/Ashey_Barott_Lab_Notebook/blob/main/posts/2025-11-06-Nematostella_spawning.md). 
- After an hour, briefly spin down the larvae, remove the PFA and add 25% ethanol for 5 mins. 
- Repeat the ethanol wash steps with 50%, 75% and 100% ethanol. 
- Store at 4C in 100% ethanol. 

### Blocking and primary antibody addition 

- Rehydrate the fixed larvae by washing them in 75%, 50% and 25% ethanol diluted in PTX for 5 mins at RT on a shaker at low speed. 
- Wash the larvae 3x with 1mL PTX. Each wash should be 5 mins at RT on a shaker at low speed. Briefly spin down larvae on benchtop centrifuge if needed. 
- Remove the PTX and add 1mL of blocking buffer. Incubate in blocking buffer for 2hrs at RT on a shaker at low speed. 
- At the end of the incubation, make blocking buffer with the primary antibody added. 
- Remove the blocking buffer and move the larvae into the blocking buffer + primary antibody. Incubate with primary antibody overnight at 4C on a shaker at low speed. 

### Secondary antibody addition 

- Remove the blocking buffer + antibody and wash with PTX for 10 mins at room temperature on a shaker at low speed. Repeat for a total of 5 washes. 
- At the end of the incubation, make blocking buffer with the secondary antibody added. 
	- **IMPORTANT**: Because the secondary antibody is fluorescent and light sensitive, all steps with the secondary antibody should be done in the dark. 
- Remove the blocking buffer and move the larvae into the blocking buffer + secondary antibody. Incubate with secondary antibody for 2hrs at RT on a shaker at low speed in the dark. 

### Mounting 

- Remove the blocking buffer + antibody and wash with PTX for 10 mins at room temperature on a shaker at low speed. Repeat for a total of 5 washes. 
- Transfer larvae to a slide and remove excess liquid. 
- Place one drop of mounting media directly onto the larvae. Apply coverslip carefully to avoid bubbles. 
- Let slide cure overnight in the dark at RT. 
- Seal coverslip with nail polish.
